Jeweler Kaat (Deduction)
No deduction — full gold value. Use when no kaat is agreed or you are selling back at market rate.
How raati kaat works:
For every 1 masha you bring, the jeweler deducts X raati from its value before paying you. Since 1 masha = 8 raati, deducting 1 raati per masha = 12.5% loss on that masha. Deducting 2 raati = 25% loss.

Fair range: 0–1 raati/masha (simple items). Max 2 raati for complex work.
Red flag: Above 2 raati/masha = overcharging. Above 4 = extreme scam.
How masha kaat works:
The jeweler deducts a fixed number of masha (and optionally raati) from your total gold weight, regardless of quantity.

Example: You bring 5 masha, jeweler says "1 masha kaat" → you get paid for 4 masha only.
Red flag: If kaat exceeds 10% of your total weight — that is excessive.
How % kaat works:
The simplest form — a direct percentage deducted from total gold value.

Fair range: 3–7% for standard jewelry. Up to 10% for intricate/stone-set work.
Red flag: Some jewelers quote % kaat AND raati kaat together — this is a double deduction scam. Insist on one method only.
What the jeweler is doing vs. what is correct
Tankhwa / Touch explained:
The jeweler uses a touchstone or acid test and claims your gold is lower purity than its stamp. E.g. your "22K" piece is declared "only 21K" by their test, and they pay at 21K rate.

The "76 number" (96-point system) trick:
Old system: 24K = 96 points. They say your gold is "76" which means 76/96 = 79.2% ≈ only 19K. Your 22K piece is deliberately rated as 19K — you lose 12.5% value instantly.

The fineness downgrade trick:
Your piece is stamped 916 (22K). Dealer says "it's only 875" (21K) by their test. That 1 karat difference = 4.6% price cut on the full value.

Your rights: PSQCA-hallmarked gold has legally certified purity. A jeweler's verbal touchstone claim CANNOT override an official hallmark. Demand the test be done in front of you at a registered assay center.
Solder / Tamba (copper) claim:
Jeweler says some weight is copper solder used to join pieces and won't pay gold rate for it.

What's fair: 0.05–0.2g for lightly soldered jewelry. Up to 0.3g for heavy chain links.
Red flag: Above 0.5g on small items, or any solder claim on a plain bangle/ring with no joins.

What that number actually means
PSQCA Hallmark — What to Look For
A genuine PSQCA-compliant hallmark includes:
1
Purity / fineness number — stamped as 916, 750, etc. This is the most important mark. It tells you exact gold content.
2
Karat mark — may appear as 22K or 22Kt alongside or instead of the fineness number.
3
Jeweler's mark — a code or initials identifying the registered jeweler. Registered jewelers have PSQCA-issued codes.
4
Hallmark certificate — any PSQCA-compliant jeweler should provide a written certificate on request. If they refuse — walk away.
Pakistan enforcement reality: PSQCA hallmarking is legally required but enforcement is still inconsistent across cities. In Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad — major registered jewelers comply. In smaller markets, always verify with a touchstone test at an independent assay center before buying or selling.
How to verify your gold yourself
◆
Check the stamp location: Inside ring band, near necklace clasp, on bracelet clasp, on pendant bail. Use a phone camera zoom — stamps are tiny.
◆
Match stamp to weight: Weigh the piece on a digital scale. Cross-check using this calculator. If the jeweler's price doesn't match — something is wrong.
◆
Request independent assay: Any registered gold testing lab can XRF-test your gold in minutes. Cost: PKR 200–500. Worth it for large purchases.
◆
Acid test: Gold does not react to nitric acid. A 22K piece touched with acid should show no color change. A jeweler doing this test in front of you is a good sign.
◆
Magnet test: Real gold is not magnetic. If a magnet attracts the piece strongly, it is not solid gold. Note: some clasps may be steel and will attract.
Common Jeweler Tricks in Pakistan
This page teaches you every trick dealers use so you recognize them in real time.
Trick 1 — The "76" / 96-point downgrade
Dealer says your 22K gold is "only 76" using the old 96-point scale. 76/96 = 79.2% = roughly 19K. Your gold is worth 22K rate but they pay you at 19K.
Money lost: On PKR 290,000/tola gold — they underpay by ~PKR 37,000 per tola.
Counter: Ask "76 out of how many?" The answer should be 96. Then calculate: 76/96 × 24 = 19K. Ask why hallmarked 22K gold is being rated as 19K. If they can't justify with a certified assay — refuse.
Trick 2 — Fineness downgrade (916 → 875)
Your piece is stamped 916 (22K). Dealer says "our touchstone shows 875" (21K). This is a 4.6% cut. On PKR 290,000/tola gold, that's ~PKR 12,000 per tola lost.
The trick: Their touchstone is calibrated wrong, or they are lying. A stamp from a registered jeweler is official certification.
Counter: Legally, a PSQCA hallmark stamp overrides any informal touchstone reading. Demand testing at a certified assay lab. Never accept a verbal touch claim on hallmarked gold.
Trick 3 — Double kaat (% + raati)
Dealer quotes "5% kaat" AND "1 raati per masha kaat" separately. You think these are the same thing — but they are applying BOTH deductions. Effective loss: 5% + 12.5% per masha = up to 17% total.
Counter: Ask the dealer to clarify: "Is this % kaat OR raati kaat — not both." Write it down before agreeing. Calculate both separately on this app and compare.
Trick 4 — Inflated raati kaat
Dealer says "3 raati per masha kaat." That sounds small — but 3 raati out of 8 = 37.5% loss per masha. On a 5-masha piece at PKR 290,000/tola, you lose PKR 52,000+.
Counter: Always convert raati kaat to a percentage using this app. Fair kaat is max 2 raati/masha (25%). Above that — negotiate hard or leave.
Trick 5 — Tamba (solder/copper) claim
Dealer says "2 grams is copper solder, we pay only for the rest." On a 10-gram piece, they deduct 2g = 20% of your gold value disappears under a "copper" claim.
Counter: Legitimate solder in a standard ring is 0.05–0.2g maximum. For a bangle with no joins, solder claim should be zero. Use the Solder/Tamba calculator on the Calculator page to see the real % impact.
Trick 6 — Wrong scale / tola confusion
Dealer weighs your gold on a scale set to different units, or confuses "international troy tola" (11.664g) with older local variants. Some older scales use 11.5g per tola — a 1.4% shortfall.
Counter: Official PSQCA / Pakistan standard: 1 Tola = 11.664 grams exactly. 1 Masha = 0.972g. 1 Raati = 0.1215g. Weigh on your own certified scale first. Never trust only the dealer's scale.
Trick 7 — "Current rate" manipulation
Dealer quotes a rate "per tola" lower than the Sarafa Bazar rate, claiming it's "today's buying rate" vs "selling rate." The spread can be 2–5% — sometimes legitimate but often inflated.
Counter: Check the live Sarafa Bazar rate (Karachi, Lahore) on any gold price website or app before visiting any jeweler. The buy-sell spread for 22K should not exceed 1–2%.
Trick 8 — Making charges on exchange
You bring old gold to exchange for new jewelry. Dealer applies kaat on your old gold AND making charges on the new piece AND a "difference" fee. Three deductions at once.
Counter: Calculate old gold value separately. Calculate new gold value separately. The only legitimate charges are: (1) fair kaat on old gold, (2) making charges on new piece. Any additional "difference" fee is a markup — negotiate it to zero or close.
Your Rights as a Buyer in Pakistan
✓
You have the right to weigh gold on your own scale or a third-party scale.
✓
You have the right to request a PSQCA hallmark certificate for any hallmarked piece.
✓
A PSQCA hallmark legally certifies purity — a dealer's touchstone cannot override it without a certified assay.
✓
You can request that any test (touchstone, acid) be performed in front of you.
✓
Complaint authority: PSQCA Consumer Protection Cell — file a complaint if a registered jeweler misrepresents purity.
