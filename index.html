Jeweler Kaat (Deduction)
No deduction — full gold value. Use when no kaat is agreed or selling back at market rate.
How raati kaat works:
For every 1 masha of gold, the jeweler deducts X raati of value. 1 masha = 8 raati, so deducting 1 raati/masha = 12.5% loss. Deducting 2 raati = 25% loss per masha.

Fair range: 0–1 raati/masha for simple items. Max 2 raati for complex work.
Red flag: Above 2 raati/masha = overcharging. Above 4 = extreme scam.
—
How masha kaat works:
A fixed masha amount deducted from total weight regardless of quantity.

Example: You bring 5 masha, jeweler says "1 masha kaat" → paid for 4 masha only.
Red flag: Kaat exceeding 10% of total weight is excessive.
—
How % kaat works:
A direct percentage deducted from total gold value — the simplest form.

Fair range: 3–7% standard. Up to 10% for intricate/stone-set work.
Red flag: Some jewelers quote % kaat AND raati kaat together — double deduction scam. Insist on one method only.

Tankhwa / Touch explained:
Jeweler uses touchstone and claims your gold is lower purity. E.g. your 22K piece declared "only 21K" — they pay 21K rate.

The "76 number" (96-point system):
Old system: 24K = 96 points. They say your gold is "76" = 76/96 = 79.2% ≈ 19K. Your 22K is paid at 19K rate — instant 12.5% loss.

Fineness downgrade: Piece stamped 916 (22K), dealer says "875 by our test" (21K) — 4.6% cut on total value.

Your right: A PSQCA hallmark stamp legally certifies purity. A verbal touchstone claim CANNOT override it. Demand testing at a registered assay center.
Solder / Tamba (copper) claim:
Jeweler says some weight is copper solder used to join pieces and won't pay gold rate for it.

What's fair: 0.05–0.2g for lightly soldered jewelry. Up to 0.3g for heavy chain links.
Red flag: Above 0.5g on small items, or any solder claim on a plain bangle/ring with no joins.

What is the spread?
The spread is the difference between the published market rate and what the dealer actually pays/charges. Dealers always buy lower and sell higher than market — that is their profit. The question is how much.

Fair spread (selling your gold): 1–2% below Sarafa rate. Max 3% is acceptable.
Fair spread (buying jewelry): 1–3% above Sarafa rate before making charges. Above 5% is excessive.
Red flag: Any dealer more than 5% away from Sarafa rate is either using a stale rate or padding profit. Walk to the next shop.

PSQCA Hallmark — What to Look For
A genuine PSQCA-compliant hallmark includes:
1
Purity / fineness number — stamped as 916, 750, etc. This is the most important mark. It tells you the exact gold content per 1000 parts.
2
Karat mark — may appear as 22K or 22Kt alongside or instead of the fineness number. Both mean the same thing.
3
Jeweler's mark — a code or initials identifying the registered jeweler. Registered jewelers carry PSQCA-issued identification codes.
4
Hallmark certificate — any PSQCA-compliant jeweler must provide a written certificate on request. If they refuse — walk away.
Pakistan enforcement reality: PSQCA hallmarking is legally required but enforcement varies. Major jewelers in Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad comply. In smaller markets always verify with an independent assay center before large transactions.
How to Verify Your Gold Yourself
◆
Find the stamp: Inside ring band, near necklace clasp, on bracelet clasp, on pendant bail. Use your phone camera zoomed in — stamps are tiny.
◆
Match stamp to weight: Weigh on a digital scale, cross-check in the Calculator tab. If the jeweler's price doesn't match — something is wrong.
◆
Request independent assay: Any registered XRF testing lab tests your gold in minutes. Cost: PKR 200–500. Worth it on large purchases.
◆
Acid test: Real gold does not react to nitric acid. A 22K piece touched with acid shows no color change. A jeweler doing this in front of you is a good sign.
◆
Magnet test: Real gold is not magnetic. Strong magnet attraction = not solid gold. Note: some clasps are steel and will attract — that is normal.
Common Jeweler Tricks in Pakistan
This page teaches you every trick dealers use so you recognize them in real time and can push back immediately.
Trick 1 — The "76" / 96-Point Downgrade
Dealer says your 22K gold is "only 76" using the old 96-point scale. 76 ÷ 96 = 79.2% = roughly 19K. Your gold is worth 22K rate but they pay you 19K.
Money lost: On PKR 290,000/tola gold — they underpay by ~PKR 37,000 per tola. Use the Hallmark Guide tab decoder to expose any number they quote.
Counter: Ask "76 out of how many?" Answer should be 96. Then: 76 ÷ 96 × 24 = 19K. Ask why PSQCA hallmarked 22K gold is being rated 19K. No certified assay proof? Refuse and walk away.
Trick 2 — Fineness Downgrade (916 → 875)
Your piece is stamped 916 (22K). Dealer says "our touchstone shows 875" (21K). That is a 4.6% cut. On PKR 290,000/tola gold, that is PKR 12,000+ per tola simply vanishing.
The lie: Their touchstone is calibrated wrong — or they are deliberately lying.
Counter: A PSQCA hallmark stamp legally overrides any informal touchstone reading. Demand testing at a certified assay lab. Never accept a verbal touch claim on hallmarked gold.
Trick 3 — Double Kaat (% + Raati Together)
Dealer quotes "5% kaat" AND "1 raati per masha kaat" separately. You assume it's the same thing described two ways — it's not. They apply BOTH. Effective loss: 5% + 12.5% per masha = up to 17% total deduction.
Counter: Ask clearly: "Is this % kaat OR raati kaat — not both." Write it down. Calculate each separately in the Calculator tab and compare the PKR difference.
Trick 4 — Inflated Raati Kaat
Dealer says "3 raati per masha kaat." Sounds harmless — but 3 raati out of 8 = 37.5% loss per masha. On a 5-masha piece at PKR 290,000/tola, you lose over PKR 52,000.
Counter: Always convert raati kaat to a percentage (raati ÷ 8 × 100). Fair max is 2 raati/masha = 25% per masha. Above that — negotiate hard or leave. Use the Raati Kaat section in the Calculator tab.
Trick 5 — Tamba / Solder Inflation
Dealer says "2 grams is copper solder, we pay only for the rest." On a 10-gram piece, they just deducted 20% of your gold's value using the word "copper."
Counter: Legitimate solder in a standard ring is 0.05–0.2g maximum. For a plain bangle with no joins, the solder claim should be zero. Use the Solder/Tamba tab in the Calculator to see the real PKR and % impact.
Trick 6 — Wrong Scale / Tola Confusion
Dealer weighs on a scale set to different units, or uses an old local tola variant. Some older scales use 11.5g per tola instead of 11.664g — a 1.4% shortfall on every transaction. Small per piece, massive over a lifetime.
Counter: Official PSQCA / Pakistan standard: 1 Tola = 11.664g exactly. 1 Masha = 0.972g. 1 Raati = 0.1215g. Weigh on your own certified scale before handing gold to any jeweler.
Trick 7 — "Today's Buying Rate" Manipulation
Dealer quotes a rate lower than the Sarafa Bazar rate, calling it "today's buying rate." The buy-sell spread can be artificially inflated to 3–5% above the legitimate spread.
Counter: Check the live Sarafa Bazar rate for Karachi or Lahore on any gold price website before visiting any jeweler. The buy-sell spread for 22K should not exceed 1–2% in normal conditions.
Trick 8 — Triple Deduction on Exchange
You bring old gold to exchange for new jewelry. Dealer applies: (1) kaat on old gold, (2) making charges on new piece, AND (3) a vague "difference" or "ghaat" fee. Three deductions at once, often none of them explained clearly.
Counter: Calculate old gold value separately. Calculate new gold value separately. The only legitimate charges are fair kaat on old gold and making charges on the new piece. Any additional "difference" or "ghaat" fee is pure markup — push it to zero.
Trick 9 — Wet Weight / Stone Weight Not Disclosed
Jewelry with stones, pearls, or enamel work — the dealer weighs the full piece including all non-gold components, then pays you the gold rate for the entire weight. Stones can account for 10–30% of a piece's weight.
Counter: Always ask: "What is the gold weight only, excluding stones?" Insist they remove visible stones or apply a discount for stone weight. For expensive pieces, ask for a separate assay of the gold component only.
Your Rights as a Buyer / Seller in Pakistan
✓
You have the right to weigh gold on your own scale or any third-party scale before transacting.
✓
You have the right to request a PSQCA hallmark certificate for any piece sold as hallmarked.
✓
A PSQCA hallmark stamp legally certifies purity. A dealer's verbal touchstone cannot override it without a certified independent assay.
✓
You can request any touchstone or acid test be performed in front of you, not behind a counter.
✓
You can file a complaint with the PSQCA Consumer Protection Cell if a registered jeweler misrepresents purity or weight.
✓
You can always walk away. A legitimate jeweler will not pressure you or suddenly lower kaat when you leave.
