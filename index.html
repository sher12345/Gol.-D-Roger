PSQCA Hallmark — What to Look For
1
Purity / fineness number — stamped as 916, 750, etc. Legally certified gold content per 1000 parts.
2
Karat mark — may appear as 22K or 22Kt alongside the fineness number.
3
Jeweler's mark — PSQCA-issued identification code of the registered jeweler.
4
Hallmark certificate — any PSQCA-compliant jeweler must provide this on request.
Legal force: A PSQCA hallmark stamp legally certifies purity. A verbal touchstone claim CANNOT override it. Demand testing at a registered assay center.
How to Verify Your Gold Yourself
◆
Find the stamp: Inside ring band, near necklace clasp. Use phone camera zoomed in.
◆
Request independent assay: XRF testing labs: PKR 200–500. Worth it on large transactions.
◆
Acid test: Real gold does not react to nitric acid. No color change on 22K.
◆
Magnet test: Real gold is not magnetic. Strong attraction = not solid gold.
Common Jeweler Tricks in Pakistan
Every trick dealers use — so you recognize them in real time and push back immediately.
Trick 1 — The "76" / 96-Point Downgrade
Dealer says your 22K gold is "only 76" using the old 96-point scale. 76 ÷ 96 = 79.2% = ~19K. Your gold is worth 22K rate but they pay you 19K. Loss: ~PKR 37,000 per tola on a 290,000/tola market.
Counter: Ask "76 out of how many?" Then: 76 ÷ 96 × 24 = 19K. Ask why PSQCA hallmarked 22K gold is rated 19K. No certified assay proof? Refuse and walk away.
Trick 2 — Fineness Downgrade (916 → 875)
Your piece is stamped 916 (22K). Dealer says "our touchstone shows 875" (21K). That is a 4.6% cut. On 290,000/tola gold = PKR 12,000+ per tola simply vanishing.
Counter: A PSQCA hallmark stamp legally overrides any informal touchstone. Demand testing at a certified assay lab.
Trick 3 — Double Kaat (% + Raati Together)
Dealer quotes "5% kaat" AND "1 raati per masha" separately — applies BOTH. Effective loss: up to 17% total deduction.
Counter: Ask clearly: "Is this % kaat OR raati kaat — not both." Write it down before agreeing.
Trick 4 — Inflated Raati Kaat
Dealer says "3 raati per masha kaat." 3 raati out of 8 = 37.5% loss per masha. On a 5-masha piece = over PKR 52,000 lost.
Counter: Always convert: raati ÷ 8 × 100 = % loss. Fair max is 2 raati/masha = 25%. Above that — negotiate or leave.
Trick 5 — Tamba / Solder Inflation
Dealer says "2 grams is copper solder." On a 10-gram piece = 20% of your gold's value lost to the word "copper."
Counter: Legitimate solder: 0.05–0.2g maximum. Zero on plain bangles/rings with no joins.
Trick 6 — Wrong Scale / Tola Confusion
Scale set to different units. Some older scales use 11.5g per tola instead of 11.664g — 1.4% shortfall on every transaction.
Counter: Official Pakistan standard: 1 Tola = 11.664g exactly. Weigh on your own certified scale first.
Trick 7 — "Today's Buying Rate" Manipulation
Dealer quotes a rate lower than Sarafa Bazar, calling it "buying rate." Spread artificially inflated to 3–5% above legitimate spread.
Counter: Check live Sarafa rate on gold.pk before visiting. Buy-sell spread for 22K should not exceed 1–2% normally.
Trick 8 — Triple Deduction on Exchange
You exchange old gold for new. Dealer applies: (1) kaat on old gold, (2) making charges on new piece, AND (3) vague "ghaat" fee. Three hidden deductions.
Counter: Calculate old and new gold separately. The only legitimate charges are fair kaat + making charges. Any "ghaat" is pure markup.
Trick 9 — Stone Weight Not Disclosed
Dealer weighs the full piece including stones. Stones can be 10–30% of total weight. You get paid gold rate for stones.
Counter: Ask: "What is the gold weight only, excluding stones?" Demand stones be removed or discounted. For expensive pieces, request separate assay of gold only.
Your Rights as a Buyer / Seller in Pakistan
✓
You have the right to weigh gold on your own scale or any third-party scale before transacting.
✓
You have the right to request a PSQCA hallmark certificate for any piece sold as hallmarked.
✓
A PSQCA hallmark stamp legally certifies purity. A dealer's verbal touchstone cannot override it without a certified independent assay.
✓
You can request any test be performed in front of you, not behind a counter.
✓
You can file a complaint with the PSQCA Consumer Protection Cell if a registered jeweler misrepresents purity or weight.
✓
You can always walk away. A legitimate jeweler will not pressure you.

Jeweler Registration Requirements
▸
Every jeweller manufacturing or supplying jewellery must be registered with the Sales Tax Collector under Section 14 of the Sales Tax Act.
▸
Registered jewellers must issue tax invoices in English or Urdu for every sale.
▸
Monthly sales tax returns must be filed. A jeweller who cannot show you a tax invoice on request is likely unregistered or non-compliant.
▸
A zargar (labour-only craftsman who does not sell directly) is exempt from registration.
AML / Anti-Money Laundering Rules for Gold
Cash transactions over Rs. 2,000,000 (20 lakh) — both buyer and seller must comply with AML/CFT rules under Pakistan's Anti-Money Laundering Act.
!
CNIC required: For cash gold transactions above Rs. 2 million, the jeweler must verify your identity (CNIC) and keep records. You must provide it. This is law.
!
CTR filing: Cash transactions of Rs. 2 million+ must be reported by the jeweller to FIU-Pakistan as a Cash Transaction Report (CTR).
!
STR filing: Any suspicious transaction must be reported regardless of amount — no minimum threshold.
!
Splitting is illegal: Breaking one large deal into multiple smaller transactions to avoid the Rs. 2 million threshold is called "structuring" — it is a criminal offence under the AML Act.
!
Penalty: Money laundering conviction = up to 10 years imprisonment + fine up to twice the value of property involved.
!
Record keeping: All transaction records must be kept for minimum 5 years by the jeweller.
PSQCA — Gold Purity Law
▸
Pakistan Standards and Quality Control Authority (PSQCA) sets purity standards for all gold sold in Pakistan.
▸
International fineness numbers (916, 750 etc.) are legally required hallmarks on all jewellery.
▸
A PSQCA hallmark certificate must be provided on request for hallmarked pieces.
▸
A dealer's verbal touchstone claim CANNOT legally override a PSQCA hallmark without a certified independent assay lab report.
▸
Misrepresentation of purity or weight is a consumer rights violation under Pakistan law — file a complaint with the PSQCA Consumer Protection Cell.
Sarafa Associations — Official Rate Sources
▸
Karachi Sarafa Association — sets the national benchmark daily rate. All city rates follow Karachi.
▸
All Punjab Gem Merchants & Jewellers Association (APGMJA) — sets rates for Punjab (Lahore, Multan, Faisalabad, Rawalpindi).
▸
Digital sources updated from associations: gold.pk, sarafa.pk, urdupoint.com/gold, forex.pk/bullion — all receive rates directly from Sarafa associations.
▸
Rate frequency: Published once or multiple times per day based on international gold movement (XAU/USD spot price).
▸
City rates: Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad, Multan, Peshawar, Quetta, Rawalpindi, Faisalabad usually match — small variations due to local dealer premiums only.
Note: Associations publish rates — they are not government regulators. The CCP (Competition Commission of Pakistan) has noted that rate-setting by associations lacks full transparency and is pushing for reforms.
Import / Export Rules
▸
Import of precious metals and export of gold jewellery is allowed under the Precious Metals and Jewellery Order 2013 (formerly SRO 760).
▸
All import/export transactions must go through TDAP (Trade Development Authority of Pakistan) using a Jewellery Passbook.
▸
Entrustment Scheme transactions must be processed exclusively through the same bank that handled the corresponding import.
▸
Gold export registration requires recommendation from APGMJA or All Pakistan Commercial Exporters Association.
